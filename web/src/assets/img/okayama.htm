Okayama

The city is located in the south-western part of the Japanese island of Honshū. Okayama offers tourists a lot of sights and interesting locations. There you will find one of the three most famous Japanese gardens Kōraku-en. This picturesque garden with tea houses, canals, ponds, and small bridges was built at the end of the 17th century. Together with the castle of the 16th century, they make an incredibly harmonious ensemble. The walls of the castle are black, earning it the nickname Crow Castle. It was destroyed due to American bombings during World War II, but in 1966 it was rebuilt. Only the “moon-viewing” tower survived. There is a Museum of samurai heritage in the castle. Also, you should visit the Okayama Prefectural Museum of Art, the Hayashibara Museum of Art, the Yumeji Art Museum, and the Okayama Orient Museum. The city can boast of the largest Symphony Halls in the country.
